TYPES OF DIABETES IN CHILDREN:

Type 1 diabetes occurs when pancreas produces little or no insulin. The children need insulin to survive, so the diabetic children need to be provided with insulin through an external source with injections or with an insulin pump. It the most common type among children and can be called as the most common chronic childhood disease. Type 1 disorder in children is known as juvenile diabetes or insulin dependent diabetes.

Relatives are at risk of getting diabetes:

Cousins are threat of getting diabetes. Close relatives of a person with type 1 diabetes are at threat of developing diabetes. According to MSD MANUALS, brothers and sisters have about 4 to 8% risk and identical twins been at much greater risk 30 to 50%. The risk of a child with type 1 who has a diabetic father is about 10% and is 4%if the mother is diabetic.

How would you know if your child is suffering from diabetes type 1?

The sign and symptoms that children usually develop during this disease are:

      Increased thirst

      Frequent urination

      Irritability or behavioral changes

      Fatigue and weakness

      Unintentional weight loss

      Fruity smelling breath

If your child develops these signs or symptoms, its time to see a doctor. If the symptoms are not recognized as being caused by diabetes and treated, children may, develop diabetic ketoacidosis.

 

CAN BABIES BE BORN WITH DIABETES 2?

Type 2 diabetes occurs when the cells in the body dont respond adequately to insulin which is called insulin resistance. This lack is frequently cited to as a comparative insulin inadequacy as against to the exact shortage seen in type 1 diabetes. Very rarely, babies are born with diabetes type 2 and it is called neonatal diabetes. It is caused by a problem in gene. It fades away with time when child is 12 months old, but the diabetes usually returns later in life.

Signs and symptoms of type 2:

This type of diabetes has milder symptoms than type 1 and develop more slowly. These include:

  Increased thirst (polydipsia) high blood sugar level causes increased thirst.

  Increased urination (polyuria) urinating more often than usual at night.

  Increased hunger (polyphagia)

COMPLICATIONS OF DIABETES IN CHILDREN AND ADOLESCENTS:

Diabetes causes immediate complications and long- term complications. Life-long complexities are mostly because of:

      Social and psychological issue

      Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) (Wisse)

      Blood vessel problems.

INSULIN DELIVERY METHODS:

Insulin can be injected in several ways:

  Vial and syringe

  Insulin pen

  Insulin pump

Some children use vial and syringe. In this method, each dose of insulin is drawn up into a syringe from a vial and is injected under the skin, usually in the arm, thigh or abdominal wall. The amount of insulin in the syringe will hold varies depending on the amount of insulin needed per injection. Young children often use syringes with unit markings to allow for smaller adjustments in the doses of insulin to be used.

An insulin pen, is a convenient way to use insulin for many children. The pen contains a cartridge that holds enough insulin for several doses. It is not a painful process too.

 

Photo by Artem Podrez from Pexels

Insulin pumps are small, computerized devices that mimic the way the human pancreas delivers small doses of short acting insulin continuously (basal rate). The pumps can be programmed to give different amounts of insulin at different times of day and night. Insulin pumps may be used with continuous glucose monitoring systems.
