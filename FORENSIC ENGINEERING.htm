FORENSIC ENGINEERING:

Forensic engineering is the practical application of engineering principles to the investigations of failures or detection of faults in performance. Forensic engineering also involves testimony on the evidences of these investigations before court of law.

PURPOSE OF FORENSIC ENGINEERING:

  Develop procedures to minimize the number of failures

  Provide guidelines for conducting failure investigation

  Develop practice for a better investigation

  Provide guidelines for ethical conduct in forensic engineering

  Disseminate information of the failures and their causes

TYPES OF FAILURE:

Failure is not always associated with structure. It can make a structural deficient or dysfunctional in usage. It can have secondary adverse effects as follows:

  SAFETY FAILURES:

This can be due to injury, death or even risk to people. Collapsing of framework during concrete placement, trench collapse, slip and fall over wet floor, punching shear failure in flat slab concrete floor are included in the safety failures.

  FUNCTIONAL FAILURES:

Compromise done in intended usage can lead to these functional failures: Excessive vibration of floor, poor air conditioning, roof leakage and poor acoustics.

  ANCILLARY FAILURE:

Adverse effects on schedule, cost, or use. The use of extra money and disturbance of the budget is a big failure during construction. Delayed construction, unexpected foundation problems, unavailability of materials, strikes and natural disasters are included in this type of failure.

 

WHAT IS THE FAILURE BEHIND TACOMA NARROWS BRIDGE COLLAPSE?

The Tacoma Narrows Bridge was built in 1940 which spanned in Tacoma Narrows straits. It is the historical name given to the twin suspension bridge. It collapsed just after 4 months due to aeroelastic flutter. In a very short time, it was noticed that the bridge has started to dangerously buckle and sway along its length during windy conditions. Even with the normal winds, it started undulating noticeably. This was worrisome for engineers. Many engineers started conducting experiments in a tunnel on the structural behavior of bridge when subjected to wind loads.

FINDINGS OF THE FORENSIC ENGINEERS:

On the day, Tacoma Bridge collapse, it experienced winds of about 19m/s (i.e about 70kmph). The center stay was torsionally vibrating at frequency of 36cpm (cycles per minute) in nine different segments. The amplitude of these torsional oscillations got so high steadily, that the convulsions tore several suspenders lose, and the span broke up. The investigation disclosed that the bridge could not bear the heavy gust of wind. At the same time, the narrow two-lane roadway gave the span a high degree of flexibility. These combined effects made the bridge highly vulnerable to aerodynamic forces which were not understood completely at that time. What lessons did forensic engineers learn from this failure? The small community of suspension bridge engineers believed that lighter and narrower bridges were theoretically and functionally sound. The great bridges cost too much. They presented immensely complicated problems of engineering and construction. But it was called a blind spot and this was a root problem. The best suspension bridge designers believed that earlier failures had occurred because of heavy traffic loading and poor workmanship. They considered negligence of the engineers as the cause of failure and not the wind pressure.
